INTRODUCTION The two datasets chosen are MathAchieve and MathAchSchool. Both relate Achievement Scores in Mathematics to different variables which have been the focus of study for many articles in the modern world. MathAchieve contains the School number, status of being a Minority, Sex of the individual, Socioeconomic Status (SES), a numeric vector of mathematics achievement score (MathAch), and a numeric vector of the mean SES for the entire school. MathAchSchool contains the School number, Size of the school, Sector (whether the school is Public or Catholic), a numeric vector giving the percentage of students on the academic track/academic performance (PRACAD), a numeric vector measuring the discrimination climate (DISCLIM), a disciplinary factor with levels 0 and 1 (HIMINTY, not used in the Project below), and a numeric vector giving the mean SES scores (MEANSES). The individuals and schools were common to both datasets. They were acquired from the package (“nlme”) from R itself. This is an interesting topic, esepcially considering my own background as a mathematics/sciences Tutor for many organizations. I aspire to find links between different variables and student performance. A negative correlation can be expected between academic performace (PRACAD) or Mathematics Achievement Scores (MathAch) and discrimination climate in school (DISCLIM). Similarly, a negative correlation can be found between PRACAD and SES, and PRACAD and MathAch. Being a minority can also be expected to lead to lower PRACAD and MathAch values. An addition of another variable called Average Achievement Score will be added as an indicator of the mean of all scores at a given school. It is important to note, especially in modern society, how being a minority affects students’ performance in schools. This study can be further extrapolated to study the psychological effects behind and the idea of self-fulfilling prophecy.
